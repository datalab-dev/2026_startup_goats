# Team Agreement 

*Disclaimer: This is a living document subject to change in the future.*

## Roles 
**Document Manager:** Rash
The document manager will be responsible for tracking and organizing the diversity of documents that the team is using. This also extends to all of our tools, such as GitHub, Slack, and Google Drive. In addition, this person is responsible for setting up and maintaining the overarching file structure for the code, figures, deliverables, and other aspects of the project. 

**Representative:** Camila 
The representative will be responsible for speaking for the group in group settings. This includes presenting findings, asking questions on behalf of the group, and expressing the standing and general consensus of the group as appropriate. The representative is responsible for representing the team during discussion sessions, full team meetings, and presentations. 


**Meeting Facilitator:** Del & Hilary 

The meeting facilitator will be responsible for drafting task agendas, leading meeting discussions, and ensuring the team stays focused on project priorities. The facilitator will create actionable items based off meetings with subject matter experts and Datalab personnel in order to prepare for the next.

**Scheduling:** Justin

The scheduler will be responsible for coordinating meeting times with students, Principal Investigator, and technical leads. This will include creating and maintaining a group calendar, sending student reminders about meetings, tracking deadlines on Slack’s to do list. The scheduler will follow up on action items to ensure that the team will stay on schedule. 


**Notes:** All, combined
All team members are expected to take notes during full team meetings. Team members will then post their share of notes to a shared document. As there are multiple people taking notes, this will result in a lower probability of important points being missed during meetings. Notes will also be taken during student meetings and questions that are noted will be raised on Issues on Github by the end of Thursday. 

## Communication Expectations

There will be 2 main channels of communication that this team will use: the main Slack channel, 2026_smith_goat, and a separate channel consisting of only student members for smaller questions. Timely responses are expected within 24 hours on Slack. Additionally, we will utilize GitHub Issues for any other problems. 

### Communication Through Slack

Communication on Slack will take place through two avenues: 

- Through the main Slack channel:
	- We will utilize the main Slack channel to ask for support when stuck during individual work time, and for general questions pertaining to scheduling or deadlines. 
	- We will update the main Slack channel with important technical updates regarding pull requests and when to merge to the main branch.
- Through a student group channel:
	- The student group channel will mainly be used between students to divide up work after each in-person meeting, and post-communication. 
	- If a student is unable to attend a meeting, due to conflict or illness, the group should be notified, and other members will complete that student’s individual portion of work.
		- The absent student is then responsible for making up an equivalent for their portion of missed work in the future when they return. 

### Communication through GitHub Issues

All project-related questions and 'need-to-know' points for DataLab or Smith Goat Team should be logged in GitHub Issues. In addition, any changes to the code, whether it’s a work in progress (denoted by [**WIP**]) or ready for merge (denoted by [**MRG**]) should be signified in the title for the pull request. This will be tracked for any changes, acceptance or denial by the principal investigators. 

### Communication Through Google Docs

For document-based systems that require communication, such as this Team Agreement document, the student team agrees to use clear communication methods within Google Docs, such as commenting and suggesting, to organize ideas and ensure everyone’s contributions are clear and of high quality. 

## Accountability

Team members are expected to update the to-do list in the 2026_smith_goat slack channel with what they are working on as tasks come up. This section will be updated on a consistent basis to ensure project alignment with technical leads and principal investigators. 

Additionally, if team members experience a scheduling conflict, they must inform the rest of the team members. Lastly Slack will also contain a masterlist containing the division of labour, bookmarked documents that are commonly referred to for team members to fall back on. 

## Assessment of our Team:
As stated by Colton, the assessment portion of our team is graded through individual code which will be committed to the 2026_startup_goats GitHub repository. These will include issues created, overall participation, and whether team members are hindering the work of others.

## Values and Skills
Our team values clear communication, attention to detail, and adaptability. The team is expected to take initiative and be understanding, patient, and respectful of others. Skills that the team will utilize are the R programming language and creating visualizations in R, as well as being able to learn how to use new applications should the need arise.
